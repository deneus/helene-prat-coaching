# Logo Concepts — The We Knows

**Date :** 2026-04-11
**Statut :** Brainstorm livré, 10 concepts documentés, rendus visuels à générer dans Nano Banana 2
**Marque :** The We Knows (voir `docs/designs/2026-04-11-naming-orsc-we.md`)

---

## Contexte

- **Nom** : The We Knows
- **Métier** : ORSC (Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching) — coache le système relationnel comme entité à part entière ("Third Entity"), pas les individus
- **Mantra** : *"The system has its own answers. Reveal the system to itself."*
- **Signature linguistique** : la "faute" grammaticale *"we knows"* (au lieu de *we know*) est volontaire — elle traite le **We** comme entité singulière
- **Audiences** : couples + équipes + partenariats pro
- **Ton recherché** : premium, sobre, chaleureux sans être cheesy, **pas therapy-coded**
- **Typo existante du site** : Cormorant Garamond (serif éditorial) + Mulish (sans humaniste) — le logo peut s'intégrer à ce pair ou le transcender

## Brief logo

Critères du naming, transposés au visuel :
1. Pas cheesy
2. Punchy, reconnaissable instantanément
3. Pas therapy-coded (wellness / yoga studio / self-help)
4. Mémorisable
5. Pas générique (pas le 1000e logo de coach)
6. Dit le métier sans tagline (système relationnel, le "we", ORSC)

## 🚫 Les 8 clichés visuels bannis (Devil's Advocate)

1. **Cercles Venn / intersection** — PowerPoint HR 2010
2. **Mains entrelacées / qui se touchent** — connotation AA, caritatif
3. **Lotus / mandala / fleur ouverte** — yoga studio, spa
4. **Arbre / racines / branches** — family therapy 101
5. **Silhouettes humaines stylisées** — ONG, mutuelle santé, consulting RH low-cost
6. **Flèche ascendante / courbe qui monte** — coaching business/performance ; contredit le mantra ORSC
7. **Papillon / transformation / chrysalide** — self-help Instagram
8. **Infini / ruban de Möbius / boucle** — couple therapy cliché + Meta/Facebook qui a pollué la forme

**Bonus à éviter** : dégradés pastel bleu-violet, typos manuscrites "amicales", point sur le i transformé en cœur/étoile.

## Les 10 concepts

### 1. The Singular We · *typographic*

**Description visuelle.** Wordmark deux lignes. *"the we"* en petit sur la première ligne, *"knows."* en plus grand sur la seconde. Le "we" est souligné d'un trait fin qui fusionne les deux lettres en une seule unité graphique. Point final terracotta = "le système a parlé".

- **Typo** : GT Sectra Display (alt : Canela Deck)
- **Palette** : `#0E1116` ink · `#F4F1EC` cream · `#B8462E` terracotta
- **Risque** : sans le trait sous "we", banal wordmark d'agence

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a minimal typographic logo for a premium coaching brand. The wordmark reads exactly "the we knows" set on two lines: "the we" appears in smaller size on the top line, and "knows." appears larger on the bottom line below. Use a refined serif display typeface similar to GT Sectra Display or Canela Deck. All letters are lowercase. Draw a thin horizontal line beneath the two letters "we" that visually fuses them into a single unit — not a decorative underline, a structural connector. The period after "knows" is a solid filled circle in terracotta (#B8462E). The rest of the text is ink black (#0E1116) on a warm cream background (#F4F1EC). Editorial luxury-publication aesthetic, generous negative space, centered composition. Output as a clean square 1:1 image, vector-style, no textures, no gradients, no shadows.
```

### 2. The Quiet Mark · *symbole + wordmark*

**Description visuelle.** Cercle creux traversé par une barre horizontale qui déborde légèrement des deux côtés — "un système traversé par quelque chose qui le dépasse" (la Third Entity qui déborde des individus). À droite, wordmark "The We Knows" en grotesque suisse neutre.

- **Typo** : Söhne / Neue Haas Grotesk Display
- **Palette** : `#141414` ink · `#EDE8DE` linen · `#3B5A4A` forest green
- **Risque** : la barre peut être lue comme "no entry" si mal proportionnée

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a minimal corporate logo: a small symbol paired with a wordmark in horizontal lockup. The symbol is a thin-lined hollow circle pierced horizontally by a solid bar; the bar extends slightly beyond both edges of the circle. To the right of the symbol, the wordmark reads exactly "The We Knows" in a clean Swiss grotesque sans-serif (Söhne or Neue Haas Grotesk Display), horizontally aligned, tight but readable tracking. Colors: ink black (#141414) for the wordmark and the circle outline, forest green (#3B5A4A) for the piercing bar, on a cream linen background (#EDE8DE). Flat geometric design, no gradients, no shadows. Corporate premium aesthetic. Output as a square 1:1 image, vector-style, centered with clear negative space on all sides.
```

### 3. The Knowing Line · *typographic — top pick craft*

**Description visuelle.** Logotype une ligne. Le `k` de **Knows** porte une **jambe custom étirée** qui glisse sous `n-o-w-s` et les souligne. Typographiquement, le `k` tient tout le verbe — c'est le `k` qui affirme que "we" est un sujet singulier. Une respiration, pas un swash.

- **Typo** : PP Editorial New Ultralight (Pangram Pangram)
- **Palette** : `#111111` ink · `#F4EFE6` cream · `#8A1B1B` accent (optionnel)
- **Risque** : à <24px la jambe custom s'écrase en tache

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design an editorial logotype for a premium brand. The text reads exactly "The We Knows" on a single horizontal line, sentence case (only the T is capital, everything else lowercase). Use a refined ultralight editorial serif like PP Editorial New Ultralight. Apply tight negative kerning so the line feels compact. The signature detail: the lowercase "k" in "Knows" has a custom extended descender. The leg of the k sweeps downward below the baseline and then horizontally underlines the remaining letters "n", "o", "w", "s" — acting as a structural underline embedded in the typography, not a decorative swash. The underline must end cleanly after the s. Colors: ink black (#111111) on warm cream (#F4EFE6). Swiss editorial design, Pentagram-quality craftsmanship. Output as a square 1:1 image, vector-style, white background, centered.
```

### 4. Double-U · *monogramme*

**Description visuelle.** Monogramme `WE` où la barre médiane du `E` **partage** littéralement la pointe centrale du `W`. Deux lettres qui fusionnent par un trait commun. En négatif, un `M` fantôme apparaît. Lock-up : micro-caps `THE WE KNOWS — ORSC` sous le monogramme.

- **Typo** : custom sur squelette Migra Extrabold (+ Söhne Mono / ABC Diatype Mono pour tagline)
- **Palette** : `#0B0B0C` obsidian · `#EDE7DA` bone · `#B4411F` rust
- **Risque** : œil non averti peut lire "ME" ou pictogramme abstrait

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a custom monogram logo merging the two capital letters "W" and "E" into a single ligature. The letters share one structural stroke: the central pointed peak of the W serves as the horizontal middle bar of the E. Build the letterforms on the skeleton of an extra-bold geometric serif like Migra Extrabold, optically square proportions. The merged mark reads "WE" in positive space; in the negative space between the strokes, a ghost "M" is visible. Below the monogram, in very small spaced uppercase monospace letters (Söhne Mono or ABC Diatype Mono), render exactly: "THE WE KNOWS — ORSC". Colors: obsidian (#0B0B0C) for the monogram and tagline, bone white (#EDE7DA) background, with a single rust red accent (#B4411F) as a small dot between "KNOWS" and "ORSC". Mucho studio minimalist style. Output as a square 1:1 image, vector-style, centered.
```

### 5. The Period · *détail signature — top pick système*

**Description visuelle.** Logotype `the we knows.` en bas de casse italique, avec un **point final cercle parfait surdimensionné** (2,5× la taille d'un point normal). Le point EST la marque autonome : en favicon, avatar, dorure de couverture, seul le point apparaît. Un oracle graphique.

- **Typo** : Reckless Neue Light Italic (Displaay)
- **Palette** : `#0A0A0A` near-black · `#F2ECE0` cream · `#D6412B` vermillion
- **Risque** : le point seul, sans contexte, ne signifie rien au début

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a wordmark logo centered on one signature detail. The full text reads exactly "the we knows." in all lowercase italic serif. Use Reckless Neue Light Italic or a similar contemporary italic display serif. The final period must NOT be the default font glyph — replace it with a perfectly geometric solid circle, 2.5 times the size of a standard period, aligned to the baseline. This oversized period is saturated in vermillion (#D6412B) and is the brand's iconic element. The rest of the text is near-black (#0A0A0A) on a cream background (#F2ECE0). Swiss minimalist typography, editorial elegance, Pentagram craftsmanship. Output as a square 1:1 image, vector-style, clean background, centered composition.
```

### 6. The Third Dot · *conceptuel*

**Description visuelle.** Wordmark `the we knows` lowercase. Au-dessus de "we", **trois points** : un sur le `w`, un sur le `e`, et un **troisième décalé entre les deux, plus haut et légèrement plus gros**. Le cerveau cherche 2 points, en trouve 3 → micro-dissonance cognitive qui force la mémorisation. La Third Entity rendue visible.

- **Typo** : GT Sectra Fine / Söhne Buch lowercase
- **Palette** : `#1A1A2E` deep ink · `#E8E4D9` bone · `#D4574A` signal coral
- **Risque** : si le 3e point est trop discret → lu comme un tréma cassé

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a minimalist wordmark logo that reads exactly "the we knows" in all lowercase. Use a refined serif like GT Sectra Fine with tight tracking. Above the word "we" (not above the whole phrase — only over "we"), render three small dots arranged in a shallow triangular formation: two small ink-black dots sit directly above the letters w and e respectively, and a third larger dot — in signal coral (#D4574A) — floats slightly higher and is offset between the first two, not aligned to any letter. The third dot is visibly bigger than the other two. The rest of the wordmark is deep ink (#1A1A2E) on a warm bone background (#E8E4D9). Minimal, iconic, high-recall vector logo. Output as a square 1:1 image, clean white background, centered.
```

### 7. The Bonded S · *ligature*

**Description visuelle.** Wordmark `we knows.` une ligne. Le `s` final est **étiré en arc** et se reboucle sous le `w` de "we", formant une soulignure-laisse. Le `s` pluriel d'un verbe singulier revient embrasser le `We`. Le logo **explique la faute grammaticale** visuellement.

- **Typo** : Reckless Neue Medium / Canela Deck
- **Palette** : `#0E2A2B` forest night · `#F5F1E8` cream · `#C89B3C` brass
- **Risque** : la boucle devient de la bouillie en favicon 16px (prévoir un lockup réduit)

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a wordmark logo that reads exactly "we knows." on a single horizontal line. Use Reckless Neue Medium or Canela Deck (contemporary serif with moderate contrast). The signature detail: the final letter s in "knows" does not terminate normally. Its tail extends outward into a long swooping curl that loops backward underneath the entire word and comes to rest beneath the w of "we", forming a ligature-style connector that visually links the end of the phrase to its beginning. A bold solid period follows the s before its curl begins. Colors: dark forest green (#0E2A2B) for the text and curl, cream background (#F5F1E8), optional brass accent (#C89B3C) for the period. Vector-style, clean typography. Output as a square 1:1 image, white background, centered.
```

### 8. The Dot Knows · *favicon-first*

**Description visuelle.** **Point plein rouge signal** décalé dans un carré invisible — un "pixel conscient". À côté en lockup horizontal, wordmark `the we knows` lowercase, le "we" surligné par le même point-signature. Le point EST le logo. Optimisé pour survivre à 16×16 et pour pop dans un feed LinkedIn saturé de bleus corporate.

- **Typo** : Neue Haas Grotesk Display
- **Palette** : `#0A0A0A` black · `#FFFFFF` white · `#E63946` signal red
- **Risque** : mal cadré → lu comme bouton "rec" ou notification

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a favicon-first logo identity. The primary mark is a single solid signal-red dot (#E63946), centered inside an imaginary square with slight intentional offset from absolute center. In horizontal lockup to the right of this mark, render a wordmark reading exactly "the we knows" in all lowercase Neue Haas Grotesk Display. The word "we" is in regular weight; the word "knows" is in medium weight (to emphasize the intentional grammatical signature). The same red dot from the primary mark is repeated smaller, placed directly beneath the w of "we" as an accent. Colors: deep black (#0A0A0A) for the wordmark, pure white (#FFFFFF) background, signal red (#E63946) as the only accent. Ultra-clean vector identity, Swiss design, scalable from 16×16 favicon up. Output as a square 1:1 image.
```

### 9. Third Entity (n.) · *anti-consensus académique*

**Description visuelle.** Le logo est une **entrée de dictionnaire**. Serif éditorial, `the we knows` bas de casse, suivi d'une barre verticale, d'une transcription phonétique `/ðə wiː noʊz/`, et d'un `n.` italique (= *noun*). Zéro pictogramme, zéro couleur. Aesthetic Oxford English Dictionary / NYRB. Le logo dit *"nous sommes un mot qu'on définit"*, pas *"nous sommes des gens qui t'aident"*.

- **Typo** : Lyon Display / Tiempos Headline / New Spirit
- **Palette** : `#0A0A0A` ink · `#F3EEE4` off-white paper
- **Risque** : illisible en favicon 16px, peut sembler prétentieux si le reste de la marque ne suit pas

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a logo that mimics an entry from a prestigious printed dictionary (Oxford English Dictionary aesthetic). Use a classic editorial serif like Lyon Display, Tiempos Headline, or New Spirit. On a single horizontal line, render exactly this sequence: the phrase "the we knows" in lowercase roman, followed by a thin vertical pipe character "|", followed by the phonetic transcription between forward slashes "/ðə wiː noʊz/" in slightly smaller size, followed by another thin vertical pipe "|", and finally the italic abbreviation "n." (meaning noun). Everything is pure black ink (#0A0A0A) on an off-white paper background (#F3EEE4) with very subtle paper grain texture. No pictograms, no decorative elements, no color. The layout must read as a lexicographic entry extracted from a printed dictionary page. Output as a horizontal 3:2 image, clean background, editorial typography only.
```

### 10. [sic] · *anti-consensus méta — top pick différenciation*

**Description visuelle.** Le mark est une paire de crochets droits contenant `sic` en italique serif — la notation éditoriale classique qui signale *"oui, c'est volontaire, ne corrigez pas"*. Lock-up : `the we knows [sic]`. Le `[sic]` peut vivre seul comme monogramme. Encre noire, papier, rien d'autre. Le logo **assume** explicitement la grammaire cassée au lieu de la cacher.

- **Typo** : Tiempos Text Italic / Lyon Italic
- **Palette** : `#000000` pure black · `#F2EDE3` cream paper
- **Risque** : exige un public qui connaît `[sic]` — hors cible éditoriale c'est cryptique

**Prompt Nano Banana 2 :**
```
Design a minimalist logotype that is a single editorial proofreading mark. The mark consists of exactly these characters: an opening square bracket "[", followed by the lowercase italic letters "sic", followed by a closing square bracket "]". Use a classic book italic serif like Tiempos Text Italic or Lyon Italic. This "[sic]" mark is the entire brand and stands alone — it can function as a complete logo. Below the bracketed mark, in slightly smaller lowercase roman (same font family), render the optional lockup: "the we knows [sic]". Colors: pure black ink (#000000) on a cream paper background (#F2EDE3). No other colors, no illustrations, no ornaments. Aesthetic: New York Review of Books, academic journal, editorial annotation tradition. Output as a square 1:1 image, centered composition with generous whitespace around the mark.
```

## 🥇 Top 3 à générer en premier

1. **🏆 The Knowing Line** (#3) — le plus **défendable devant un DA**. Le `k` qui tient `nows` sous sa jambe résout *typographiquement* la friction du nom. Craftsmanship Pentagram-level, vieillit sur 10 ans.
2. **🥈 The Period** (#5) — le plus **scalable comme système d'identité**. Tout l'univers visuel tient dans un cercle qui vit en favicon, dorure, tampon, dégradé CSS. Un pari sur la durée.
3. **🥉 [sic]** (#10) — le plus **différenciant**. Aucun autre coach n'a ce second degré. Risqué commercialement (cryptique pour cible B2B corporate) mais inoubliable pour ceux qui captent.

## Tips d'usage Nano Banana 2

1. **Colle un prompt à la fois** — NB2 donne 4 variantes par run, choisis la meilleure puis itère
2. **Pour itérer** : *"Keep the same composition but change the red to deep blue (#1B3A6B)"* — NB2 comprend les retouches
3. **Pour tester contraste** : *"Same logo but now rendered in white on a black background"*
4. **Si le texte est mal rendu** (rare sur NB2) : ajoute *"Render the text exactly as written, spelled correctly: [the exact text]"* au début
5. **Pour export vectoriel** : NB2 sort du raster — génère en **2048×2048 minimum**, puis vectorise via `vectorizer.ai` ou dans Illustrator
6. **Pour batcher** : tu peux demander *"Generate four variations of this logo, each with a different accent color from this palette: #B8462E, #3B5A4A, #D6412B, #C89B3C"*
